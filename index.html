Manga history

Manga is the form in which Japanese comics or comics are known. The creator of the term "manga" (漫画) was
Hokusai Katsushika, a renowned painter of the Japanese ukiyo-e movement .
This painter combined the kanjis for the word informal (漫 man) and drawing (画 ga) which could be translated
in the most literal sense as “informal drawings” or “doodles”. On the other hand, the author who is in
charge of illustrating or drawing the manga is known as mangaka . However, in the Western world, the word is
used to refer very specifically to the Japanese style of illustration and specifically Japanese comics.

First comics (1790 - 1887)

Article 1:

It is presumed that the first comics were created around the year 1790, and originated due to the arrival of
people from the West to Japan. This style of drawing, however, became very popular among the Japanese. The
first steps of the manga are tied to two types of traditional artistic expressions: Japanese graphic art
dating from the XI and the indisputable influence of the world of Western comics, which had already won its
place in the entertainment medium during the 19th century.
